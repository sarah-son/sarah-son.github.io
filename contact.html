Conclusion

Have questions? I have answers.

How did we do it?

We started off with a data set from Kaggle and started evaluating it. Then we created different models using the parameters such as, age, sex, maximum heart rate, etc., and chose the best ones to put on our website. Using these models, we were able to predict if a person will or will not have heart disease by recording the accuracy of our models. Afterwards, we created this website to present our models with information to create a heart disease predictor.

Future Improvements

With this heart disease predictor, people will be able to predict whether or not they will have heart disease by inputting parameters such as their age, sex, or resting blood pressure. To further improve the heart disease predictor, we can collect more data to bring light to different results. We can also try different algorithms to improve the predictor as a whole. Deeper research can also be conducted to have a better understanding of the predictor, and possibly find any errors, or important signs signifying if a person is likely to get heart disease.
